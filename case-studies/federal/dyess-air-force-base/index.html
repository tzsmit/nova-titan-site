The Challenge

Dyess Air Force Base, home to the 7th Bomb Wing and B-1B Lancer bomber operations, required secure HP enterprise equipment removal and disposal as part of ongoing IT infrastructure modernization. The project demanded strict adherence to Department of Defense facility security protocols, military equipment handling standards, and zero tolerance for operational disruption on an active Air Force installation.

Key Requirements:

DoD Security Compliance

Full compliance with federal facility access protocols, security clearance requirements, and DoD equipment handling procedures

Zero Mission Disruption

Equipment decommissioning could not interfere with active military operations or compromise base security during execution

Multi-Party Coordination

Seamless coordination required between Nova Titan, prime contractor Core Technology Solutions, and Dyess AFB security personnel

Rural West Texas Location

Supporting military readiness in an underserved rural region where specialized IT contractor availability is limited

Our Solution

Nova Titan Systems executed a carefully planned equipment decommissioning operation that prioritized security, compliance, and mission continuity. Our team navigated complex DoD facility procedures while maintaining the professional standards expected of federal IT contractors supporting active military installations.

Implementation Approach:

1

Pre-Project Security Coordination

Completed all required base access protocols, security clearance verifications, and facility entry procedures. Coordinated with Dyess AFB security personnel and prime contractor Core Technology Solutions to ensure seamless project authorization and scheduling.

2

DoD-Compliant Equipment Handling

Executed HP enterprise equipment removal following strict Department of Defense standards for federal facility operations. Maintained complete chain of custody documentation and equipment disposal protocols per military specifications.

3

Zero-Disruption Execution

Completed all decommissioning activities with zero impact to active Air Force operations. Worked within designated timeframes and facility constraints to ensure mission-critical military operations continued uninterrupted.

4

Comprehensive Documentation & Closeout

Provided complete project documentation including equipment disposal records, security compliance confirmations, and federal facility procedures adherence. Ensured all paperwork met DoD contractor requirements for federal installation work.
